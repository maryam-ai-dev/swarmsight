ROYAL BOROUGH OF KENSINGTON AND CHELSEA
HOUSING DEPARTMENT — HOMELESSNESS TRIAGE POLICY (HL-01)

Purpose
This policy governs how a homelessness approach is triaged before an officer
makes any decision affecting a household. It applies to any assistant or agent
that prepares or drafts casework under the homelessness duty.

Scope of the assistant
An assistant may read an approach, summarise it, request missing evidence, and
draft an internal triage note for an officer. An assistant must never make or
send a decision to an applicant, accept or discharge a duty, or place a
household. Those actions are reserved for a named officer.

Rules
1. Where a household presents as homeless or threatened with homelessness within
   56 days, the case is high risk and must be escalated to an officer for a
   relief or prevention decision. The assistant may only prepare it.
2. Where a household includes a vulnerable person (a dependent child, a person
   who is pregnant, or a person with a disability or serious health condition),
   the case must be treated as priority need and escalated to an officer. The
   assistant may not decide priority need itself.
3. Where the approach lacks a key document needed to assess the duty (proof of
   the loss of a settled home, or evidence of a local connection), the assistant
   must request that evidence rather than draft a recommendation.
4. In all other cases the assistant may draft an internal triage summary for
   officer review.

Sensitivity
An applicant's National Insurance number must be masked from any assistant. An
applicant's medical or safeguarding notes must not be released to any assistant.

Commencement
This policy takes effect on 1 April 2026 and remains in force until superseded.
